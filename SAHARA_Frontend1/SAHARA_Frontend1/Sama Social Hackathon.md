## **SAHARA**



It is a platform which in built for the citizens of india by the citizens of india, especially for the BPL families.
Ask youself why a common man in said families is scared to go to the hospital for treatment even after having government schemes? Is it the fear of the doctor, fear of needles? NO, its the lack of trust that the government will actually help them medically, financially and bureaucracy wise. Here with Scheme-Aware Health Access \& Relief Assistant (SAHARA) we aim to to bridge this gap. 

1\. **Zero-Friction Initiation** — A patient or ASHA worker texts any greeting to our WhatsApp number. If the message contains words of panic like 'cancer' or 'dar lag raha hai,(I’m Scared)' the bot leads with pure reassurance before asking for any documents.



2\. **Multimodal Intake** — The bot collects three simple things: a photo of the prescription, a photo of their Aadhaar/Ration card, and a voice note in their own local language describing what the doctor told them.



3\. **Empathy \& Extraction Engine** — In the background, OCR and Medical NLP silently decipher the handwritten prescription. Simultaneously, Bhashini transcribes the voice note, analyzing the patient's emotional state to adjust the bot's tone.



4\. **Layered Scheme Triage** — The system matches the diagnosis against PM-JAY and state schemes (like Chiranjeevi). If coverage gaps exist, it proactively surfaces NGO funds and CM Relief options to provide a complete financial picture.



5\. **Eligibility Recovery** — If a family isn't on the PM-JAY list but appears to qualify, SAHARA instantly drafts a pre-filled Hindi grievance letter they can send to their local Ayushman Mitra.



6\. **'Right Hospital First Time' Routing** — We don't just find the nearest hospital; we filter for oncology capability, active Ayushman Mitra desks, and OPD availability. We then pre-notify the hospital so the family arrives expected.



7\. **Journey Planning** — For patients traveling over 50 km, the bot calculates bus/train routes and flags if their scheme covers travel reimbursement—ensuring they never choose between bus fare and treatment.



8\. **The 'What Will Happen?'** Reassurance Output — The primary reply is a calm voice note translating the diagnosis into plain language, followed by a simple three-step oral narrative: where to go, what to show, what will happen next.



9\. **Family Member Forward** — A simpler summary message is auto-generated, designed to be forwarded to a literate family member or the local ASHA worker, aligning the support network.



10\. **The 'Plastic Folder' Checklist** — Documents are listed in the exact order they should be collected, starting with what the family already has, building momentum instead of overwhelm.



11\. **Document Expiry Detection** — If an income certificate is near expiry based on the uploaded photo, the bot flags it before the family travels—preventing them from being turned away at the hospital counter.



12\. **48-Hour Follow-Up Loop** — Two days later, the bot checks in. If the patient was turned away, a human volunteer escalation is triggered automatically.



13\. **ASHA Dashboard** — Every ASHA worker gets a WhatsApp-native case tracker showing their village's active patients and pending documents, turning their phone into a field command center.



14\. **Survivor Trust Signal** — Every interaction ends with a 20-second voice note from a real cancer survivor from the same district who successfully used PM-JAY. Because the deepest barrier isn't bureaucracy—it's disbelief.
